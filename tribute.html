Mahatma Gandhi

The Father of the Nation

Mahatma Gandhi was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule, and in turn inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Timeline

  • 1869: Born in Porbandar, India
  • 1888: Traveled to London to study law
  • 1915: Returned to India and joined the Indian National Congress
  • 1930: Led the Salt March to protest British salt tax
  • 1942: Launched the Quit India Movement
  • 1947: India gained independence
  • 1948: Assassinated in New Delhi

Quotes

"Be the change that you wish to see in the world."

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others."
